McKinsey-Style Research System
Use this reference when the task is not just "collect information" but produce industry research
that can survive a management meeting.
Operating Principle
Research is argument construction, not information collection. Start with a falsifiable answer,
then test whether the evidence, market math, competitive landscape, and user reality support it.
Core Flow
Day-1 Answer: write the provisional conclusion before deep research.
SCQA: compress situation, complication, question, and answer into one clear argument.
Ghost Deck: write the full title-only storyline before visual design.
Evidence collection: gather only what affects the core hypothesis.
Decision pages: build the pages that decide the argument, not decorative background pages.
Skeptical verification: recalculate key numbers and pressure-test assumptions.
Final deck/report: turn the verified argument into a readable executive artifact.
Fifteen Methods To Enforce
Pyramid Principle: the answer comes first; the rest of the report proves it.
SCQA: situation, complication, question, and answer must be explicit.
Ghost Deck: finish the title outline before designing slides.
Horizontal logic: reading only the titles must form a complete argument.
Vertical logic: each title must be proven by the body content below it.
Action titles: every title states a conclusion, not a topic label.
MECE: categories should avoid overlap and important omissions.
Hypothesis-driven research: Day-1 Answer is written early; research can overturn it.
80/20: investigate the variables that can change the answer.
Elevator test: the core thesis should stand alone in 30 seconds.
Bottom-up sizing: TAM/SAM/SOM and unit economics come from explicit formulas; top-down is only
a cross-check.
Good/Better/Best: use one tiering logic across competition, product, and customer segments.
Competitive reaction pressure test: ask how major players would respond if the thesis works.
"So What?" discipline: every exhibit must explain why it matters.
Honesty rule: optimize for credibility, not optimism.
Ghost Deck Rules
Before writing prose or making visuals, create:
$0 The Answer: one governing conclusion plus 3-4 supporting pillars.
Section titles: each one is a sentence-length conclusion.
Decision pages: market size, competition, economics, user pain, timing, and execution risk.
Kill pages: the evidence or assumptions that could invalidate the recommendation.
Delete pages whose titles cannot connect into a coherent SCQA argument.
Decision Page Standards
A decision page must include:
conclusion title;
formulas and units for key numbers;
assumptions labeled as source-backed or judgment calls;
source IDs beside important facts;
implication or recommendation;
caveat or kill condition when relevant.
Honesty Protocol
If research overturns the Day-1 Answer:
state what changed;
show the evidence that caused the change;
update the answer;
preserve the caveat in the final output instead of hiding it.
