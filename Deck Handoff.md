Use this when the user wants the keyword research turned into a McKinsey-style deck, PDF, or
executive presentation.
Pairing With mckinsey-deck
If mckinsey-deck is available, use this skill for research and synthesis, then use
mckinsey-deck for the visual HTML/PDF deck. Do not duplicate the deck engine here.
Recommended Deck Storyline
Cover: industry/topic and one italic subtitle.
$0 The Answer: governing thought plus 3-4 pillars.
SCQA: why this question matters now.
Keyword architecture: 5-8 categories and what they reveal.
Market sizing: bottom-up TAM/SAM/SOM with formulas and assumptions.
Demand and signal evidence: user, company, policy, technology, and market signals.
Competitive landscape: heatmap or Good/Better/Best tiers.
Economics: pricing, gross margin, payback, or ROI where relevant.
Head people map: experts, creators, builders, analysts, and what to learn from them.
Expert interview agenda: the questions worth asking insiders.
Project directions: safe/core, aggressive/early, and watchlist options.
Reverse execution plan: 7/30/60/90-day actions, metrics, and kill criteria.
Source appendix.
Slide Headline Rule
Every slide headline should be a conclusion, not a topic label.
Weak:
"ChatGPT 100 keywords"
"Industry trends"
Strong:
"ChatGPT is shifting from a chat product into a workflow and platform layer"
"The strongest project opportunities sit where adoption is high but workflow ownership is still unsettled"
Research-To-Deck Mapping
Collective thesis sentence -> executive answer slide.
5-8 categories -> architecture or divider slides.
Keyword definitions -> appendix or dense table slides.
Trend score -> decision slide.
Project directions -> table slide.
7/30/60/90 plan -> decision or table slide.
Sources -> clickable source appendix.
QC Gate
Before delivery, verify:
every slide renders without overlap or footer collision;
source IDs are continuous and cited where claims appear;
executive summary claims match decision-page numbers;
all core numbers in the deck match data.json or the equivalent source artifact;
formulas preserve units and time periods;
title-only reading forms a complete argument;
every exhibit answers "so what?";
caveats and judgment calls are visible.
If any check fails, fix the research or deck before delivery.
